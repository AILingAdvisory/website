Pillar II – Risk Management

The Detection & Control Gap

AI-native risks—such as model drift, proxy discrimination, and third-party data leakage—are invisible to traditional operational risk frameworks. Most firms lack the modern controls or testing methods to meaningfully detect, measure, and control these new, automated threats.

R — Control What AI Can Do

(Defense • Risk Controls • Safe Operations)

What You Get

  • A sector-tailored AI risk taxonomy covering 30–40 unique AI risks
  • A library of AI-specific controls, including bias testing, drift detection, explainability checks, and human oversight points

Pillar III – Compliance

The Assurance & Evidence Gap

Regulators (EU, MAS, HKMA, FCA) no longer accept narrative explanations. They require firms to prove that AI is safe, fair, and auditable. Without a repeatable audit trail and robust validation evidence, firms cannot meet this burden of proof.

(Assurance • Compliance • Validation)

Industry Plug-ins: Designed for Your Sector

Financial subsectors face unique AI risk pressures. Our plug-ins provide tailored insights, controls, and risk considerations for your industry.

Banking

  • Algorithmic Fair Lending: Managing "proxy discrimination" risk in credit models (e.g., digital redlining).
  • Legacy System Constraints: Fragmented data in silos cripples data governance and increases integration risk.
  • Strategic Deposit Disruption: Threat from agentic AI autonomously moving deposits, eroding net interest margin.

Wealth Management (incl. Robo-Advisors)

  • The "Fiduciary in the Machine": Proving algorithmic advice fulfils regulatory "Duty of Care" (suitability) and "Duty of Loyalty."
  • Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) Liability: Risk of advisors "rubber-stamping" flawed AI advice, leading to scalable mis-selling.
  • Suitability from Incomplete Data: GRC failure from models basing advice on simplistic client questionnaires.

Asset Management (Buy-Side / Quant)

  • Model Epistemology ("Overfitting"): Existential risk of models learning historical "noise" vs. a true "signal."
  • Alpha Decay & Non-Stationary Markets: GRC becomes a continuous monitoring function as models decay in shifting markets.
  • Alternative Data Governance: Managing compliance and ethical risks (provenance, privacy) of ingesting novel "alt-data."

Insurance

  • Actuarial vs. Unfair Discrimination: Preventing AI underwriting from using proxies for discriminatory pricing.
  • Claims Automation Explainability: Requirement to provide clear, auditable justifications for AI-driven claims denials.
  • Catastrophic Risk Modelling Failure: High risk of models failing to predict "black swan" events (e.g., climate-driven).

FinTech

  • Indirect Regulatory Scrutiny: Immense compliance burden from bank partners (e.g., OCC, FDIC) who are held accountable.
  • Multi-Jurisdictional Maze: Prohibitive cost of navigating fragmented, country-by-country licensing to scale.
  • Data Scarcity & Model Risk: High risk of model "overfitting" due to lack of deep, longitudinal historical data.

Capital Markets (Sell-Side)

  • Algorithmic Conflicts of Interest: Proving AI-generated research is objective and not biased by investment banking.
  • Public Reputational Risk: High-stakes risk of AI "hallucinations" in public research, damaging brand credibility.
  • Information Barrier Integrity: Risk of GenAI breaching legal "information barriers" by aggregating private-side data.
